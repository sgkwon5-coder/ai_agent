01 Company Overview

Origins

The formula was developed in May 1886 by pharmacist John Stith Pemberton; The Coca-Cola Company was incorporated in 1892.

Headquarters

Atlanta, Georgia, USA — operating in the Food, Beverages & Tobacco / Nonalcoholic Beverage Manufacturing industry.

Public Listing

Traded on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker "KO"; a component of the Dow Jones Industrial Average, S&P 500, and S&P 100.

Leadership

James Quincey, currently CEO, transitions to Executive Chairman effective March 31, 2026. Henrique Braun, currently EVP and COO, becomes CEO at that time.

Operating Segments

Europe, Middle East & Africa · Latin America · North America · Asia Pacific · Bottling Investments · Global Ventures.

03 Financial Highlights — FY2025

$47.9B Net Revenues

Up 2% year-over-year; organic revenue growth of 5%.

$3.04 Full-Year EPS

Up 23%, on global value share gains in ready-to-drink beverages and emerging-market performance in Brazil and Central Asia.

+38% Operating Income Growth

Comparable currency-neutral operating income up 13%; Q4 included a $960M non-cash impairment charge tied to the BODYARMOR trademark.

$7.4B Cash Flow from Operations

Free cash flow (non-GAAP) of $5.3 billion for the full year.

4–5% / 7–8% 2026 Outlook

Guidance projects organic revenue growth of 4–5% and comparable EPS growth of 7–8%.

Figures are drawn from the company's Q4 / full-year 2025 earnings release (reported February 2026) and are subject to revision in subsequent SEC filings.

04 Global Strategy & Outlook

Digital Transformation

A new Chief Digital Officer role unifies digital, data, and operational excellence — aiming to improve speed and efficiency from consumer engagement to internal operations.

Innovation Infrastructure

Plans call for innovation hubs and commercial centers of excellence across all operating segments and key markets in 2026.

Sustainability — "World Without Waste"

A long-running packaging and recycling initiative; voluntary environmental packaging-collection goal timelines have been extended from 2025/2030 to 2035.

Leadership Transition

Incoming CEO Henrique Braun, succeeding James Quincey (moving to Executive Chairman), has signaled a focus on accelerating innovation and sustaining growth momentum.

Growth Trajectory

Management points to 5% organic revenue growth in 2025 as evidence of a "durable strategy" with a "long runway" for continued expansion.
